Lazarus Island

About Us

On Lazarus Island in the late 19th century, there were a number of prison confinement sheds, but they were abandoned when an inmate serving a life sentence there managed to escape. The confinement barns were completely burned by a wildfire in 1902, and the entire area's vegetation was completely devastated by a huge fire in 1914. Lazarus Island was intended to be turned into a beach resort by the government, but those plans were never carried out. Lazarus Island attracts throngs of tourists who come to enjoy the spotless white sand beach on the C-shaped lagoon. It has also been referred to as one of Singapore's best-kept secrets and one of the forgotten islands. Lazarus Island attracts beachgoers and families seeking some peaceful time to be with loved ones.
